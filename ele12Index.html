Discovery date 1755
Discovered by Joseph Black
Origin of the Name The name is derived from Magnesia, a district of Eastern Thessaly in Greece.
Allotropes

Group 2  Melting point 650°C
Period 3  Boiling point 1090°C
Atomic number 12  Electron configuration 2, 8, 2
Relative atomic mass 24.305   Density (g cm−3) 1.74  
Toxicity Non-Toxic Appearance Silvery White
Smell Radioactivity Stable

Uses
  • Useful in aeroplane and car construction.
  • Used in products that benefit from being lightweight, such as car seats, luggage, laptops, cameras and power tools.
  • Used in flares, fireworks and sparklers.
Abundance
  • Magnesium is the eighth most abundant element in the Earth’s crust.
  • It is found in large deposits in minerals such as magnesite and dolomite.
