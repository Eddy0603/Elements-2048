Discovery date 1774
Discovered by Carl Wilhelm Scheele
Origin of the Name The name is derived from the Greek 'chloros', meaning greenish yellow.
Allotropes Cl2

Group 7  Melting point −101.5°C
Period 3  Boiling point −34.04°C
Atomic number 17  Electron configuration 2, 8, 7
Relative atomic mass 35.45   Density (g cm−3) 0.002898  
Toxicity Toxic Appearance Yellow green dense gas
Smell Choking Radioactivity Stable

Uses
  • Used as disinfectant in swimming pools and drinking water.
  • Used in the production of consumer products.
  • Making PVC.
  • Used as oxidizing agent.
  • Used as a chemical weapon in World War 1.
  • Used to balance positive ions in living things.
  • Once used to make dry cleaning solvent and anaesthetic, but now strictly regulated because it causes liver damage.
Abundance
  • Not found naturally as a gas
  • Found normally as salt or other mineral .
