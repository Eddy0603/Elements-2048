Discovery date 1817
Discovered by Johan August Arfvedson
Origin of the Name The name is derived from the Greek 'lithos' meaning stone.
Allotropes

Group 1  Melting point 180.50°C
Period 2  Boiling point 1340°C
Atomic number 3  Electron configuration 2, 1
Relative atomic mass 6.94   Density (g cm−3) 0.534 
Toxicity Toxic except for small doses Appearance Silvery Grey, Metal. Soft
Smell Radioactivity Stable

Uses
  • Making rechargable batteries.
  • Making aluminium-lithium alloys for air plane and high speed train frames.
  • Lithium oxide is used for spcial glasses and ceramics.
  • Lithium chloride is used in air conditioners.
  • Lithium hydride is used as storage for hydrogen fuel.
Abundance
  • Not found naturally as a metal
  • Found in almost all igneous rocks and water springs.
