New Element Discovered


Discovery date 1766
Discovered by Henry Cavendish
Origin of the Name The name is derived from the Greek 'hydro' and 'genes' meaning water forming.
Allotropes H2

Group 1  Melting point −259.16°C
Period 1  Boiling point −252.879°C
Atomic number 1  Electron configuration 1
Relative atomic mass 1.008   Density (g cm−3) 0.000082 
Toxicity Non-Toxic Appearance Colourless
Smell Odourless Radioactivity Stable (Except 3H)

Uses
  • Fuel, generated from water by electrolysis.
  • Making ammonia for agricultural fertiliser through the Haber process and cyclohexane and methanol.
  • Remove sulfur from fuels during the oil-refining process.
Abundance
  • Most abundant element in the universe.
  • Found on Earth in water, living things.
