Discovery date 1895
Discovered by Sir William Ramsay, Per Teodor Cleve and Nils Abraham Langlet
Origin of the Name The name is derived from the Greek, 'helios' meaning sun, as it was in the sun's corona that helium was first detected.
Allotropes

Group 0  Melting point Unknown
Period 1  Boiling point −268.928°C
Atomic number 2  Electron configuration 2
Relative atomic mass 4.003   Density (g cm−3) 0.000164  
Toxicity Toxic Appearance Colourless
Smell Odourless Radioactivity Stable

Uses
  • Keep satelite instruments cool, also to cool liquid oxygen and hydrogen that powered the Apollo space veichles.
  • A helium-ion microscope that gives better image resolution than a scanning electron microscope
  • Helium is used to provide an inert protective atmosphere for making fibre optics and semiconductors.
Abundance
  • Second most abundant element in the universe.
  • Formed from alpha-particle decay of radioactive elements in the Earth.
