Discovered by Humphry Davy
Origin of the Name The name is derived from the English word 'potash'.
Allotropes

Group 1  Melting point 63.5°C
Period 4  Boiling point 759°C
Atomic number 19  Electron configuration 2, 8, 8, 1
Relative atomic mass 39.098   Density (g cm−3) 0.89  
Toxicity Non-Toxic Appearance Soft silvery metal, tarnishes very quickly
Smell Radioactivity Stable except for 40K

Uses
  • Making detergent and liquid soap.
  • Making fertilizers.
  • Used in glass production.
  • Used to balance and maintain fluid and electrolyte balance in living things.
  • 40K might be a cause of genetic mutations to humans.
Abundance
  • Seventh most abundant metal in the Earth's crust
  • Found normally in igneous rocks.
